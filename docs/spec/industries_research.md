## Distressed Real Estate - Golden Egg Data Points

### Primary Distress Categories
1. **Tax-Related Distress**
   - Delinquent property taxes
   - State/federal tax liens
   - Tax deed auctions scheduled

2. **Mortgage/Financial Distress**
   - Preforeclosure notices (Notice of Default)
   - Foreclosure filings
   - Bank-owned (REO) properties
   - Underwater mortgages

3. **Legal/Probate Situations**
   - Probate filings (death of owner)
   - Divorce proceedings
   - Bankruptcy filings
   - Court judgments

4. **Government-Owned Properties**
   - HUD homes
   - VA foreclosures
   - Local government auctions

5. **Physical/Occupancy Distress**
   - Vacant properties
   - Code enforcement violations
   - Fire damage
   - Boarded windows
   - Absentee owners
   - Vacant landlords

### Critical Data Points to Scrape

#### Public Records (Government Sources)
- **Court Records**: Foreclosure filings, divorce cases, probate filings, bankruptcy
- **Tax Assessor**: Delinquent taxes, tax liens, auction schedules
- **Code Enforcement**: Violations, citations, compliance orders
- **Recorder's Office**: Notices of Default, Lis Pendens, mechanic's liens
- **Property Records**: Owner information, mortgage data, equity position
- **Vital Records**: Death certificates (for probate leads)

#### Property Characteristics
- Vacancy indicators (utility shutoffs, mail accumulation)
- Owner age (elderly owners more likely to sell)
- Years owned (long-term owners have more equity)
- Absentee owner status
- Landlord status
- Property condition (visual inspection data)
- Flood risk zones
- HOA liens

#### Financial Indicators
- Mortgage balance vs. property value (equity calculation)
- Multiple mortgages (overleveraged)
- Loan origination date
- Interest rate (high rates = distress)
- Payment history
- Lender information

#### Social/Behavioral Signals
- Social media posts indicating desperation
- "Must sell" language
- Life event mentions (divorce, death, job loss)
- Property listing history (multiple price reductions)
- Days on market
- Zillow/Realtor comments

### Data Sources to Scrape
1. County clerk/recorder websites
2. Court case management systems
3. Tax assessor databases
4. Code enforcement portals
5. Public auction sites (Auction.com, Hubzu, RealtyBid)
6. MLS listings
7. Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin
8. Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist
9. Probate court filings
10. Bankruptcy court PACER system


## Business Loans - Golden Egg Data Points

### Qualified Lead Criteria
- **Business Age**: 1+ years in operation (minimum)
- **Annual Revenue**: $250K+ (some lenders accept $100K+)
- **Credit Score**: 620+ (owner's personal credit)
- **Cash Flow**: Steady positive cash flow
- **Industry**: Not in high-risk categories (cannabis, adult, gambling)
- **Legal Status**: Properly registered entity (LLC, Corp, etc.)

### Financial Distress Signals (High-Intent Leads)

#### Cash Flow Problems
- Constant lack of cash despite sales
- Spending more than earning consistently
- Unable to meet payroll on time
- Delayed vendor payments
- Bounced checks or declined payments

#### Debt Indicators
- High interest payments (indicates risk profile)
- Multiple lenders with personal guarantees
- Maxed out credit lines
- Merchant cash advance (MCA) stacking
- Existing loan defaults

#### Payment Behavior
- Defaulting on bills (especially HMRC/IRS)
- Extended creditor days (paying late)
- Extended debtor days (not collecting)
- Missed tax payments
- Utility shutoff notices

#### Profitability Issues
- Falling profit margins
- Revenue declining quarter-over-quarter
- Negative EBITDA
- High burn rate
- Inventory turnover slowing

#### Operational Red Flags
- High employee turnover (especially senior staff)
- Delayed financial reporting
- Sudden strategy pivots
- Office downsizing or relocation
- Equipment repossession

### Positive Opportunity Signals (Growth Financing)
- Rapid revenue growth (need working capital)
- New contract wins (need fulfillment capital)
- Expansion plans (new location, equipment)
- Inventory purchases (seasonal businesses)
- Hiring spree (need payroll financing)
- Marketing campaigns (need cash for ads)

### Data Sources to Scrape

#### Public Records
- Business registration databases (Secretary of State)
- UCC filings (secured loans, liens)
- Court judgments and liens
- Bankruptcy filings (Chapter 7, 11, 13)
- Tax liens (federal and state)
- Lawsuit databases

#### Business Intelligence
- Dun & Bradstreet business credit reports
- Experian Business credit
- LinkedIn company pages (employee count changes)
- Glassdoor reviews (employee sentiment)
- Better Business Bureau complaints
- Industry-specific databases

#### Digital Footprint
- Company website (job postings, news)
- Social media activity (desperation posts)
- Google My Business reviews
- Yelp reviews (operational issues)
- Press releases (funding announcements)
- SEC filings (for larger companies)

#### Financial Indicators
- Revenue estimates (from data providers)
- Employee count (LinkedIn, ZoomInfo)
- Office size/location (commercial real estate databases)
- Technology stack (BuiltWith, Datanyze)
- Web traffic (SimilarWeb, Alexa)
- Customer reviews mentioning payment issues
